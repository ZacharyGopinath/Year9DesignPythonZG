SDG 13
Goal: Take urgent action to combat climate change and its impacts.
What is Climate Change?
Climate change is a change in global or regional climate patterns, in particular a change apparent from the mid to late 20th century onwards and attributed largely to the increased levels of atmospheric carbon dioxide produced by the use of fossil fuels.
Why is it important?
Climate change has many negative impacts on the environment, humans, animals, and more. For example, rising temperatures leads to ice melting in the arctic, which destroys the environment. As well, worsening air quality presents safety risks for human, such as related disease of the lungs, etc. A destabilized climate can lead to droughts/floods (which affect agriculture and crop growing), and other extreme weather patterns, such as hurricanes. All of this leads to problems with the economy, further destabilizing the world.
